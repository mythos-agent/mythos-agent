## Summary

Replace the existing 7-level technical-wishlist `ROADMAP.md` with a multi-year strategic frame organized around three themes (Foundation & Depth → Autonomy & Discovery → Ecosystem & Scale), pair it with a stable `VISION.md` north star, an active 6-month `[Roadmap]` GitHub Issue for working plans, and the supporting governance, supply-chain, and sustainability artifacts that a public OSS security tool needs in 2026. Dogfood the new RFC process by passing this very change through it.

## Motivation

The pre-refresh `ROADMAP.md` was a solo-engineer technical wishlist organized as 7 capability "Levels" plus 5 implementation "Phases" sized in weeks. It served the original maintainer's planning, but did not answer the questions a contributor, sponsor, or EU CRA-compliant downstream Manufacturer brings to a public roadmap:

- *How do I contribute?*
- *Who decides things, and how?*
- *Will this stay open?*
- *What is healthy about this project?*
- *What is it explicitly **not** trying to be?*
- *Why care vs alternatives?*

Public launch (April 2026) made the gap material. The refresh closes it without abandoning the engineering content underneath.

## Goals

- Replace ROADMAP.md with a contributor-facing, multi-year strategic document that survives replanning
- Introduce a stable VISION.md that articulates the "Shedu" north star and hard non-goals
- Adopt a 2-tier roadmap pattern (stable themes + 6-month pinned working issue) modeled on Rust, FastAPI, Pydantic AI, vLLM
- Lock in OpenSSF / EU CRA / supply-chain artifacts as time-critical work (Sept 2026 deadline)
- Establish multi-phase governance evolution (BDFL → multi-maintainer → TSC) with concrete triggers, not dates
- Establish a non-cash recognition tier (Shedu Pioneers) and a drafted-but-inactive cash bounty program
- Establish a sustainability funding stack (Sponsors → Open Collective → Sovereign Tech Fund / NLnet / OSTIF)
- Establish a CHAOSS-aligned health metrics publication and a research agenda

## Non-goals

- **Not** implementing any of the engineering capabilities the roadmap describes (deterministic taint engine, knowledge graph, validated remediation, etc.) — those are tracked in the H1/H2 Goals issues and accomplished as separate engineering PRs.
- **Not** adopting open-core licensing — the License Firewall sentence in GOVERNANCE.md prevents this regardless of future commercial direction.
- **Not** raising venture capital — the funding stack explicitly omits VC.
- **Not** making dated commitments past 6 months — themes are undated; only the active pinned Goals issue carries a 6-month horizon.
- **Not** changing the project license, name, or trademark holder — those changes follow their own GOVERNANCE process and are out of scope here.

## Alternatives considered

### Alternative A — Keep the existing 7-level ROADMAP.md and add only the missing scaffolding (governance, security, etc.) around it

**Pros:** smaller diff; no risk of disrupting contributors who have read the existing roadmap.

**Cons:** the existing ROADMAP.md leads with engineering plumbing in a way that obscures strategic direction; readers cannot easily extract a 1-paragraph summary of where the project is going. Keeping it forces every new artifact to work around its framing instead of with it.

**Why rejected:** the 7-level structure was chosen for solo-maintainer planning, not contributor coordination. Layering scaffolding around it without restructuring the core would still leave us with a roadmap that doesn't answer the six contributor questions in Motivation.

### Alternative B — Adopt a single-artifact approach (only a pinned GitHub Issue, no ROADMAP.md / VISION.md)

**Pros:** matches the AI-OSS dominant pattern (Aider, Continue, Cline, Pydantic AI, LlamaIndex). Minimal maintenance burden.

**Cons:** shedu is also a security OSS — security comparators (ZAP, Trivy, Semgrep) all maintain structured ROADMAP.md docs. EU CRA-compliant downstream Manufacturers expect a stable doc to reference. Sponsors expect to read a vision before opening their wallets. A single pinned issue cannot serve those audiences.

**Why rejected:** shedu sits at the intersection of AI OSS and security OSS. The cross-category reality requires both surfaces.

### Alternative C — Defer everything except the EU CRA compliance work

**Pros:** the only truly time-critical block is the September 2026 CRA deadline. Everything else could wait.

**Cons:** The other work *enables* the compliance work. Without GOVERNANCE clarity, the OpenSSF Best Practices Badge fails. Without SECURITY.md SLAs, the CRA stance is hollow. Without VISION non-goals, the threat model has no scoping anchor. The compliance work doesn't stand alone.

**Why rejected:** false economy. Decoupling the time-critical work from the surrounding scaffolding means rebuilding the surrounding scaffolding under deadline pressure later — exactly the wrong moment.

## Drawbacks

- **Diff size.** This refresh touches 24 files across 6 commits. Reviewers cannot read it in a single sitting.
- **Documentation surface.** Adding 14 new doc files commits the project to maintaining them. If any of them goes stale and conflicts with code reality, the gap is now public.
- **"Shedu" framing risk.** Even with the disclaimer, the name *Mythos* invites comparison with Anthropic's proprietary product. If shedu fails to ship Phase 2 capabilities, the gap between framing and reality grows visible.
- **OpenSSF Badge timeline pressure.** The Q3 2026 target is optimistic for a single-maintainer project. Slipping it is recoverable; missing it without an explanation isn't.
- **Funding-stack expectations.** Publishing a sustainability stack invites the question "did any of those funders actually say yes?" Setting up the stack does not guarantee inflows.

## Adoption plan

This RFC is itself the adoption plan for the listed artifacts. Tracking:

- The H1 2026 Goals issue carries the work that operationalizes what this RFC ships (e.g., the deterministic taint engine, OpenSSF Badge submission, Sigstore signing on the next release).
- Quarterly checks: did the Goals-issue items advance? Did the metrics in `docs/health-metrics.md` improve?
- Annual checkpoint: does a fresh reader of the project understand the strategic direction in 5 minutes? Is the contributor on-ramp <15 minutes from "I want to help" to "first PR open"? If not, refresh.
